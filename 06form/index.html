History of Kevin Kaarl

During his childhood and adolescence, Kevin actively engaged in various choirs and musical bands. At the age of 7, he became a part of a youth band in Meoqui, Chihuahua. Throughout elementary school, he joined the school choir, and later on, he became a member of another band in his hometown. In 2014, he took a break from playing the guitar and singing to embark on a photography and film project. Before initiating his musical venture, Kaarl played the guitar, with his twin brother Bryan contributing as a backup vocalist, showcasing their musical synergy during live performances.

Mexican folk singer and composer Kevin Eduardo Hernández Carlos, professionally known as Kevin Kaarl, was born on May 15, 2000. He gained recognition after releasing his debut single "Amor viejo" on September 18, 2018. The song quickly captured the public's attention, amassing over 7 million views on YouTube. In December of the same year, he skyrocketed to fame with his hit single "Vámonos a marte", which garnered over 23 million views on YouTube and 104 million streams on Spotify. This success was largely attributed to its viral spread on social media platforms.

Kevin Kaarl's two studio albums, "Hasta el fin del mundo"(2019) and "París, Texas"(2022), along with the EP "San Lucas" (2019). His popular songs include "San Lucas," "Vámonos a marte" ("Let's go to Mars"), "Colapso" ("Collapse"), "Es que yo te quiero a ti" ("I love you"), "Si supieras" ("If you only knew"), "Toda esta ciudad" ("This whole city"), "Amor viejo" ("Old love"), "Abrazado a ti" ("Hugging you"), "Mujer distante" ("Distant Woman"), "Tu si eres real" ("You're real"), and "Cómo me encanta" ("How I love it"). Several have achieved Top 10 status in different countries, with "Vámonos a marte" reaching number one on the Top 40 Charts in Chile and "San Lucas" claiming the top spot on the Youtube Top 100 Songs charts in Mexico.

NPR tiny desk concert series

Kevin Kaarl's growing recognition is underscored by his participation in NPR Music's Tiny Desk concert series. The Chihuahuan artist brings a fresh perspective to his indie-infused vocals, seamlessly blending folksy trumpet rhythms with twangy banjo. This unique fusion transforms traditional Sinaloan instrumentation into a style reminiscent of American folk bands.
